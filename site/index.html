THE PERFECT ACOUSTICS

HOW WALLACE SABINE CHANGE ACOUSTICS FOREVER

The first application of modern scientific methods to architectural acoustics was pioneered by the American physicist Wallace Sabine, the founder of the field of study. He applied the science to the Fogg Museum lecture room and later applied it to the world-famous Boston Symphony Hall. Using a simple stopwatch, Sabine tested the acoustics of the lecture hall in the Fogg Art Museum utilizing sound and the number of seat cushions, creating an equation for reverberation time. Sabine was the first to consider the materials in a concert/lecture hall and factor them in sound absorption. Prior to Sabine, architects and designers worked purely on intuition.
